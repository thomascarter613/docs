# Publishing Cadence

The Publishing Cadence defines how often Applied Innovation Corp should turn internal learning into public material.

The goal is consistency without creating content burden that distracts from execution.

---

## Recommended cadence

| Cadence | Output | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Daily or near-daily | Short founder note or observation | Preserve visibility and learning momentum |
| Weekly | One useful post, lesson, or build update | Convert weekly review into public insight |
| Monthly | One deeper essay or strategic update | Turn monthly theme into durable authority asset |
| Quarterly | Larger narrative asset | Summarize doctrine, positioning, or platform progress |

---

## Daily publishing

Daily publishing should be light.

Use short posts to share:

- what was learned
- what changed
- what problem keeps appearing
- what belief was clarified
- what pattern emerged

Do not force daily content when there is no useful insight.

---

## Weekly publishing

Weekly publishing should come from Weekly Reviews.

Good weekly posts include:

- lessons from building
- market observations
- founder reflections
- implementation insights
- AI governance notes
- workflow lessons
- product development updates

---

## Monthly publishing

Monthly publishing should come from Monthly Themes.

Monthly content should be more durable and strategic.

Use it to explain:

- what the company is learning
- what changed in the thesis
- what doctrine is emerging
- what the platform is becoming
- what buyers should understand

---

## Rule of thumb

Publish from real work.

Do not publish just to fill a schedule.
