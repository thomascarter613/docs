# Sales Pricing

Sales Pricing defines how pricing should be discussed in sales conversations.

Pricing should connect scope, value, risk, and implementation effort.

---

## Pricing principles

Pricing should be:

- clear
- scoped
- tied to business value
- simple enough to understand
- honest about what is included
- protective against uncontrolled scope
- reviewed as market evidence improves

---

## Pricing conversation structure

When discussing price, explain:

1. The problem being solved
2. The business value
3. The implementation scope
4. The delivery phases
5. What is included
6. What is excluded
7. The investment
8. The next step

---

## Pricing language

Use language like:

> Based on what we discussed, this is not a generic AI tool setup. This is an implementation project to improve a specific business workflow. The price reflects discovery, design, implementation, testing, documentation, and handoff.

---

## Scope clarity

Every price should define:

- deliverables
- timeline
- systems involved
- number of workflows
- number of revisions
- support period
- training or handoff
- exclusions

---

## Pricing review

Track pricing feedback in the Pipeline Review System.

Capture:

- objections
- hesitation
- accepted price points
- rejected price points
- value language that resonated
- scope confusion
- comparison alternatives

---

## Definition of done

Pricing is working when prospects understand what they are buying, why it costs what it costs, and what outcome the price is tied to.
