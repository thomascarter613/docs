# Discovery System

The Discovery System defines how Applied Innovation Corp learns whether a prospect has a real problem, a meaningful business need, and a suitable implementation opportunity.

Discovery is not a pitch.

Discovery is structured learning.

---

## Purpose

The Discovery System exists to understand:

- the prospect’s business
- current workflows
- manual bottlenecks
- administrative pain
- current tools
- decision process
- urgency
- budget range
- implementation readiness
- fit for Applied Innovation Corp

The output of discovery should be clarity.

---

## Discovery goals

A good discovery conversation should answer:

- What is the real problem?
- Who experiences the pain?
- How often does it happen?
- What does it cost?
- What has already been tried?
- Why now?
- Who decides?
- What would success look like?
- Is this a good fit?

---

## Discovery outputs

Every discovery process should produce:

- problem summary
- current workflow map
- automation opportunity
- risk notes
- buyer language
- objections
- qualification status
- recommended next step

---

## Qualification standard

A qualified opportunity should have:

- clear pain
- reachable decision maker
- meaningful business impact
- implementation feasibility
- urgency or strong motivation
- realistic budget
- acceptable risk
- clear next step

If these are missing, the prospect may still be useful for learning, but should not be treated as a strong sales opportunity.

---

## Definition of done

Discovery is complete when the business understands the prospect well enough to either:

- propose a clear next step
- disqualify the opportunity
- continue research
- capture useful market intelligence
