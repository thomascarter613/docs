# Discovery Scripts

These scripts provide a practical structure for discovery conversations.

They should sound natural, not robotic.

---

## Opening script

> The goal of this conversation is not for me to force a solution. I want to understand how your business currently works, where the friction is, and whether there is a real opportunity to improve the workflow with AI, automation, or better systems.

---

## Context-setting script

> I usually look for repetitive work, handoffs between tools, missed follow-ups, manual admin, scattered information, or processes that depend too much on one person remembering what to do.

---

## Workflow exploration script

> Could you walk me through how this process works today, from the moment it starts to the point where it is finished?

Follow-ups:

- Who touches this?
- What tools are involved?
- Where does information get copied or re-entered?
- Where does it usually slow down?
- What happens when it breaks?

---

## Pain clarification script

> Of everything we discussed, which part causes the most frustration, cost, delay, or risk?

---

## Value framing script

> If this were improved, what would matter most: saving time, reducing mistakes, improving follow-up, increasing revenue, making work easier for staff, or something else?

---

## Fit-check script

> Based on what I am hearing, there may be an opportunity here. Before I suggest anything, I want to make sure I understand the business impact and whether this is actually worth solving now.

---

## Closing script

> The next useful step would be for me to summarize what I heard, identify the strongest automation or workflow opportunities, and outline what an implementation could look like. That would also make clear whether this is a good fit or not.

---

## Disqualification script

> I do not want to recommend work that is not justified. Based on what we discussed, this may not be urgent enough or clear enough yet for an implementation project. I can still summarize the opportunities and what would need to change before it becomes worth pursuing.

---

## Post-call summary structure

After the call, summarize:

- business context
- main pain
- current workflow
- highest-value opportunity
- risks or unknowns
- likely fit
- recommended next step
