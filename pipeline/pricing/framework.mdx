# Pricing Framework

The Pricing Framework defines how Applied Innovation Corp designs and evaluates pricing.

Pricing should connect business value, implementation effort, delivery risk, buyer urgency, and scope clarity.

---

## Purpose

The Pricing Framework exists to:

- prevent underpricing
- reduce scope ambiguity
- connect price to value
- make offers easier to explain
- support repeatable sales conversations
- improve pricing through evidence
- protect delivery capacity

---

## Pricing principles

Pricing should be:

- outcome-aware
- scope-bound
- easy to explain
- connected to buyer pain
- protective against scope creep
- reviewed after real sales conversations
- adjusted based on evidence

---

## Core pricing inputs

| Input | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Business value | What is the outcome worth to the buyer? |
| Pain severity | How costly or urgent is the problem? |
| Scope | What exactly is included? |
| Complexity | How hard is implementation? |
| Risk | What could go wrong? |
| Access burden | How much client access or coordination is needed? |
| Reuse potential | Will this create reusable assets or platform intelligence? |
| Support needs | What happens after delivery? |

---

## Price structure

Each offer should define:

- package name
- target buyer
- problem solved
- deliverables
- exclusions
- timeline
- support period
- price or price range
- payment terms
- success criteria

---

## Pricing review questions

Ask after every sales conversation:

- Did the prospect understand the price?
- Did they object to price or value?
- Did the scope feel clear?
- Did pricing reveal a qualification issue?
- Did the offer need a smaller entry point?
- Did the buyer compare it to software, labor, consulting, or internal work?
- What language made the value clearer?

---

## Definition of done

The Pricing Framework is working when pricing conversations become clearer, more confident, and more tied to business value over time.
