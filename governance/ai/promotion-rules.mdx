# AI output promotion rules

AI output is draft material until reviewed.

These rules define when AI-generated content may become accepted documentation, doctrine, decision records, public content, or client-facing material.

---

## Core rule

AI may draft, summarize, reorganize, and recommend.

AI must not silently convert drafts into accepted doctrine.

Human verification is required before promotion.

---

## Promotion destinations

AI output may be promoted into:

- documentation pages
- templates
- checklists
- ADR drafts
- Founder Log summaries
- narrative drafts
- pipeline scripts
- delivery workflows
- governance proposals
- platform requirements

Promotion does not mean automatic acceptance.

---

## Required review

Before promotion, verify:

- facts
- source context
- assumptions
- claims
- safety
- alignment
- status
- intended audience
- related pages
- human intent

---

## Do not promote

Do not promote AI output that:

- invents facts
- makes unsupported claims
- hides uncertainty
- exposes sensitive information
- changes accepted decisions without review
- overstates maturity
- creates client commitments
- creates pricing commitments
- gives legal or financial conclusions as final
- conflicts with accepted ADRs or governance rules

---

## Promotion workflow

1. Generate draft.
2. Label as draft.
3. Review against source context.
4. Check for sensitive information.
5. Check for unsupported claims.
6. Edit into project voice.
7. Assign status.
8. Link related pages.
9. Human verifies.
10. Promote or reject.

---

## Definition of done

AI output promotion is working when AI accelerates documentation and strategy without weakening trust, safety, or decision quality.
