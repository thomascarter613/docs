# Client-safe documentation

Client-safe documentation protects client information while preserving useful business learning.

The goal is to keep lessons reusable without exposing private details.

---

## Core rule

Preserve the lesson. Protect the client.

---

## Never include

Do not include:

- client credentials
- private client data
- private client names without permission
- private financial information
- private emails
- confidential workflow details
- internal disputes
- sensitive personal information
- unapproved contract terms
- unapproved pricing details

---

## Generalization examples

| Raw detail | Safer version |
| --- | --- |
| Client company name | A local service business |
| Exact revenue | Revenue range or omitted |
| Staff member name | Decision maker or operations lead |
| Exact workflow detail | Repetitive administrative workflow |
| Exact price | Mid-four-figure implementation fee |
| Private business reason | Operational constraint |

---

## AI usage

Before sending client-related context to AI:

1. Remove credentials.
2. Remove private identifiers.
3. Remove unnecessary private context.
4. Generalize client details.
5. Mark unverified claims.
6. Include only the minimum useful context.

---

## Public usage

Before publishing client-derived lessons:

- remove client identity unless approved
- remove confidential details
- remove unsupported claims
- preserve only the generalized lesson
- avoid implying endorsement
- avoid making outcome claims without evidence

---

## Definition of done

Client-safe documentation is working when delivery lessons improve the business without exposing private client information.
