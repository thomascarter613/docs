# ADR Review Checklist

Use this checklist before accepting an Architecture Decision Record.

The goal is to make sure every accepted ADR is clear, grounded, useful, and safe to rely on.

---

## Required fields

Confirm the ADR includes:

- title
- number
- status
- date
- context
- decision
- alternatives considered
- consequences
- related documents
- supersession information, if applicable

---

## Context review

Ask:

- Is the problem clearly explained?
- Is the decision necessary?
- Is the scope clear?
- Does the ADR explain why this matters now?
- Does it identify what would happen if no decision were made?

---

## Decision review

Ask:

- Is the decision written clearly?
- Is the decision specific enough to guide implementation?
- Does it avoid vague or overly broad language?
- Does it define what is included and excluded?
- Does it create a durable rule or direction?

---

## Alternatives review

Ask:

- Were reasonable alternatives considered?
- Is the rejected path explained fairly?
- Are trade-offs visible?
- Does the ADR avoid pretending the decision has no downside?

---

## Consequences review

Ask:

- Are positive consequences identified?
- Are negative consequences identified?
- Are implementation implications clear?
- Are risks visible?
- Are future migration or reversal concerns noted?

---

## Governance review

Ask:

- Does this decision affect other docs?
- Does this decision require a roadmap update?
- Does this decision affect existing ADRs?
- Should any previous ADR be superseded?
- Does this need human verification before acceptance?

---

## AI review

If AI helped draft the ADR, confirm:

- facts were checked
- claims were verified
- uncertainty is marked
- no invented rationale was added
- the final decision reflects human intent

---

## Acceptance standard

An ADR is ready to accept when:

- the decision is clear
- the reasoning is explicit
- alternatives are documented
- consequences are acknowledged
- related docs are linked
- supersession is handled
- the ADR index can be updated confidently
