# Weekly Reviews Overview

Weekly Reviews turn Daily Founder Logs into operating intelligence.

Use this page to review one week of Founder Log entries. The weekly review turns daily activity into operating awareness. It identifies patterns, decisions, risks, repeated friction, useful assets, and next actions.

A Weekly Review is where raw execution becomes management insight.

---

## Purpose

Weekly Reviews exist to answer:

- What happened this week?
- What changed?
- What repeated?
- What decisions were made?
- What assumptions need testing?
- What risks became visible?
- What useful assets emerged?
- What should happen next?

The weekly review prevents the business from moving forward without learning from the work already done.

---

## Inputs

A Weekly Review should use:

- Daily Logs from the week
- tasks completed
- client or prospect notes
- delivery notes
- platform work
- AI Cofounder outputs
- open risks
- unresolved assumptions

The review should focus on what the week revealed, not merely what the week contained.

---

## Outputs

A completed Weekly Review should produce:

- confirmed decisions
- open assumptions
- active risks
- follow-up actions
- reusable asset candidates
- AI Cofounder handoff notes
- promotion candidates for other docs

Weekly Reviews should feed Monthly Themes.

---

## Weekly Review standard

A useful Weekly Review should identify:

- the main theme of the week
- the most important progress
- the most important friction
- the most important decision
- the most important open question
- what should be repeated
- what should be stopped
- what should be systematized

---

## Definition of done

A Weekly Review is complete when it produces a clearer operating picture than the daily notes alone.

The result should help the founder decide what matters next.
