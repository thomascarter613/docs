# Redaction Rules

Founder Log entries may contain raw context from strategy, sales, client work, personal founder reflection, platform planning, and AI-assisted execution.

Before that context is reused, published, promoted, or given to AI, it must be reviewed for sensitive information.

The purpose of redaction is to preserve the business lesson while removing unnecessary exposure.

---

## Core rule

Capture the insight. Protect the sensitive details.

A Founder Log entry should preserve what the business learned without exposing information that does not need to be reused.

---

## Information that must be removed or masked

Redact the following before reuse:

- passwords
- API keys
- access tokens
- private keys
- seed phrases
- account numbers
- Social Security numbers
- private financial details
- client confidential information
- private client names when not needed
- personal addresses
- personal phone numbers
- private legal information
- medical information
- sensitive family information
- unapproved pricing details
- unapproved contract terms
- private emails
- internal-only security details

---

## Information that may need partial redaction

Some information can be generalized instead of removed.

| Raw detail | Safer replacement |
| --- | --- |
| Client company name | A local service business |
| Exact price | A mid-four-figure implementation fee |
| Personal name | The decision maker |
| Specific location | A regional market |
| Private business issue | An operational constraint |
| Exact revenue | Current revenue range |
| Exact workflow | A repetitive administrative workflow |

---

## Safe transformation examples

Unsafe:

> Smith Family Dental said their front desk misses 14 follow-up calls per week and cannot afford the $4,500 proposal this month.

Safer:

> A local healthcare-adjacent service business showed interest in follow-up automation but raised timing and budget concerns around a mid-four-figure implementation.

Unsafe:

> The client gave us access to their Gmail and CRM credentials.

Safer:

> The implementation requires a secure credential intake process before any client system access is accepted.

Unsafe:

> I think this prospect is disorganized and probably does not understand AI.

Safer:

> The prospect may need clearer operational framing and a simpler explanation of the implementation process.

---

## AI reuse rules

Before giving Founder Log content to AI:

1. Remove secrets and credentials.
2. Remove unnecessary personal identifiers.
3. Generalize client details unless identity is required.
4. Mark unverified claims as unverified.
5. Separate facts from interpretations.
6. Remove private emotional content that does not affect business operations.
7. Include only the context needed for the task.
8. Tell the AI what not to assume.

AI should receive the minimum useful context.

---

## Public reuse rules

Before turning Founder Log content into public material:

- remove private names unless permission exists
- remove confidential client details
- remove unsupported claims
- remove exact numbers unless verified and approved
- remove anything that could embarrass, expose, or misrepresent a person or client
- convert raw frustration into professional insight
- preserve the lesson, not the private situation

---

## Client-facing reuse rules

Before turning Founder Log content into client-facing material:

- remove references to other clients
- remove internal doubts or unresolved assumptions
- remove private founder notes
- verify all claims
- confirm pricing, scope, and commitments
- make the language professional and specific
- avoid promising outcomes not yet proven

---

## Governance reuse rules

Before promoting a Founder Log entry into Governance:

- keep the decision context
- keep the evidence
- keep the risk
- remove unnecessary personal detail
- identify whether the item is a rule, standard, decision, risk, or operating doctrine
- assign review cadence

Governance needs enough context to explain the rule, but not every raw detail that produced it.

---

## Redaction labels

Use these labels when editing entries.

| Label | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `[REDACTED_SECRET]` | Secret, credential, token, or key removed |
| `[REDACTED_CLIENT]` | Client identity removed |
| `[REDACTED_PERSON]` | Personal identity removed |
| `[GENERALIZED]` | Specific details replaced with a safer category |
| `[UNVERIFIED]` | Claim requires confirmation |
| `[INTERNAL_ONLY]` | Safe internally, not for public reuse |

---

## Redaction checklist

Before reuse, ask:

- Does this contain secrets?
- Does this identify a client unnecessarily?
- Does this expose private personal information?
- Does this include unverified claims?
- Does this include emotional wording that should be reframed?
- Does the AI need this detail to complete the task?
- Could this create legal, reputational, or client trust risk?
- Can the lesson be preserved with less exposure?

---

## Rule of thumb

When the lesson can survive without the private detail, remove the private detail.

The Founder Log should make the company smarter without making it less safe.
