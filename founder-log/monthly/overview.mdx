# Monthly Themes Overview

Monthly Themes synthesize a month of Daily Logs and Weekly Reviews.

The monthly review turns operating awareness into strategy, doctrine, reusable assets, and business memory.

Daily Logs capture what happened.

Weekly Reviews identify patterns.

Monthly Themes decide what those patterns mean.

---

## Purpose

Monthly Themes exist to answer:

- What did this month teach the company?
- What strategic shift became visible?
- What assumptions were confirmed or weakened?
- What risks changed?
- What should become doctrine?
- What should become a reusable asset?
- What should guide next month?

Monthly Themes help prevent the company from repeating the same lessons without converting them into operating infrastructure.

---

## Inputs

A Monthly Theme review should use:

- all Weekly Reviews from the month
- important Daily Logs
- major decisions
- open assumptions
- active risks
- platform implications
- narrative insights
- sales and delivery lessons

---

## Outputs

A completed Monthly Theme review may produce:

- strategic doctrine
- revised priorities
- new governance rules
- reusable templates
- offer improvements
- platform requirements
- narrative assets
- knowledge graph updates

---

## Theme standard

A theme should be supported by repeated evidence.

A single event may be important, but it is not automatically a theme.

A Monthly Theme should connect multiple signals into a useful conclusion.

---

## Definition of done

A Monthly Theme review is complete when the business has a clearer understanding of what changed, what matters, and what should guide the next month.
