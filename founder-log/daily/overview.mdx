# Daily Logs Overview

Daily Logs are the primary capture layer of the Founder Log.

A daily log records what happened, why it mattered, what changed, and what should be reviewed later. It does not need to be polished. It does need to be specific enough that the future founder, a collaborator, or the AI Cofounder can recover the operating context.

The Daily Log is where raw founder execution becomes durable business memory.

---

## Purpose

Daily Logs exist to prevent useful context from being lost.

They capture:

- work completed
- decisions made
- assumptions discovered
- risks noticed
- signals from prospects, clients, or the market
- platform ideas
- delivery lessons
- narrative insights
- AI Cofounder context
- follow-up actions

The goal is not journaling for its own sake. The goal is operational continuity.

---

## What belongs in a Daily Log

Use a Daily Log when something happened that could affect:

- strategy
- sales
- delivery
- platform design
- governance
- positioning
- founder capacity
- client understanding
- future AI assistance

A useful daily entry can be short. It should be clear.

---

## Daily Log standard

Each Daily Log should answer:

- What happened today?
- Why did it matter?
- What changed?
- What did I decide?
- What assumptions appeared?
- What risks or blockers appeared?
- What should be reviewed later?
- What should the AI Cofounder remember?

---

## Relationship to weekly reviews

Daily Logs feed Weekly Reviews.

A Daily Log captures raw context.

A Weekly Review extracts patterns.

Do not try to make every daily entry perfect. Capture enough useful context so that weekly review can turn the week into operating intelligence.

---

## Definition of done

A Daily Log is complete when:

- the date is clear
- the main work is captured
- decisions are noted
- assumptions are visible
- risks are not hidden
- follow-up actions are listed
- useful AI Cofounder context is preserved

The Daily Log should make tomorrow easier.
