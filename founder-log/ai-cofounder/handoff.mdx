# AI Cofounder Handoff

The AI Cofounder Handoff defines how Founder Log context should be prepared before AI assists with strategy, planning, writing, research, delivery, governance, or platform work.

The goal is to make AI assistance grounded, useful, and safe.

The AI Cofounder should operate from recorded context, not guessed context.

---

## Purpose

The handoff exists to help the AI Cofounder understand:

- what the business is trying to do
- what changed recently
- what decisions are active
- what assumptions remain unresolved
- what risks require attention
- what constraints must be respected
- what work should happen next
- what requires human verification

A good handoff reduces hallucination, drift, repetition, and overconfidence.

---

## When to create a handoff

Create or update an AI Cofounder handoff:

- before starting a major planning session
- after a weekly review
- after a monthly review
- before generating client-facing material
- before making strategic recommendations
- before converting logs into tasks or docs
- after major decisions
- when context has become scattered
- when resuming work after interruption

## Context hierarchy

When giving the AI Cofounder context, prioritize:

1. Current request
2. Current handoff
3. Recent daily logs
4. Latest weekly review
5. Latest monthly theme review
6. Active decisions
7. Open assumptions
8. Active risks
9. Governance rules
10. Older background context

Recent context should override older context unless a canonical decision says otherwise.

---

## Human verification rules

The AI Cofounder must not be treated as final authority for:

* legal conclusions
* financial commitments
* client-facing promises
* public claims
* sensitive personal information
* hiring or employment decisions
* security decisions
* governance changes
* major strategic pivots
* final pricing decisions

The AI Cofounder may draft, analyze, summarize, and recommend. The founder approves.

---

## Anti-hallucination instructions

Every handoff should remind the AI Cofounder:

* Do not invent facts.
* Mark uncertainty clearly.
* Separate facts from interpretation.
* Ask for missing context only when absolutely necessary.
* Prefer grounded recommendations.
* Identify assumptions.
* Identify risks.
* Do not treat draft notes as final decisions.
* Do not expose private information.
* Do not create false certainty.

## Example handoff

```md
## Current business context

Applied Innovation Corp is building an AI-native business combining AI implementation consulting, governed developer tooling, and a reusable operating platform.

## Current priority

Clarify the Founder Log system and turn founder memory into structured business infrastructure.

## Recent progress

- Founder Log overview was drafted.
- Recommended subpages were identified.
- Daily, weekly, and monthly review structure was selected.

## Active decisions

- Founder Log is a structured operating record, not a casual journal.
- Daily logs feed weekly reviews.
- Weekly reviews feed monthly themes.
- Durable insights are promoted into other documentation systems.

## Open assumptions

- The Founder Log can become a reliable context layer for the AI Cofounder.
- Regular review will produce reusable assets.
- The structure is simple enough to maintain.

## Active risks

- Logging could become too heavy.
- AI may overinterpret draft entries.
- Sensitive information may be reused without redaction.

## Constraints

- Keep the system founder-friendly.
- Avoid unnecessary bureaucracy.
- Preserve useful context without overloading daily execution.

## Do not assume

- Draft entries are final decisions.
- Unverified claims are true.
- AI-generated recommendations are approved.

## Needs human verification

- Any public-facing claim.
- Any strategic decision.
- Any client-facing commitment.

## Requested AI role

Documentation architect and founder operations strategist.

## Desired output

Final MDX pages for the Founder Log system.

## Success criteria

Pages are clear, useful, copy/paste-ready, and consistent with the docs structure.
```

---

## Handoff quality checklist

A handoff is ready when it includes:

* current context
* current priority
* recent progress
* active decisions
* open assumptions
* risks
* constraints
* do-not-assume instructions
* human verification requirements
* desired output
* success criteria

A good handoff lets the AI Cofounder continue from reality instead of reconstructing context from fragments.
