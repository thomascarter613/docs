# Logging Standard

This page defines the standard for Founder Log entries.

The purpose of the Founder Log is not to capture everything. The purpose is to capture the operating context that helps Applied Innovation Corp make better decisions, preserve founder reasoning, assist the AI Cofounder, and turn execution into reusable business infrastructure.

---

## Core standard

A Founder Log entry is useful when it is:

- specific
- honest
- dated
- grounded in context
- separated into facts and interpretations
- safe to reuse
- reviewable later
- connected to possible next actions

A useful entry should help someone understand what happened, why it mattered, and what should happen next.

---

## Minimum viable entry

At minimum, a Founder Log entry should include:

- date
- summary
- work completed
- decisions made
- assumptions discovered
- risks or blockers
- next actions
- AI Cofounder context

Short entries are acceptable. Vague entries are not.

---

## Facts vs interpretations

Separate facts from interpretations.

A fact is something observed or recorded.

Example:

> A prospect asked whether AI automation would reduce administrative time without requiring new software training.

An interpretation is a conclusion drawn from facts.

Example:

> This may indicate that our offer should emphasize operational relief more than technical sophistication.

Both are useful. They should not be confused.

---

## Evidence standard

Important claims should be tied to evidence.

Good evidence includes:

- direct prospect or client statements
- repeated objections
- observed workflow friction
- implementation results
- cost, time, or error measurements
- internal execution data
- documented decisions
- links to commits, issues, docs, or artifacts

Weak evidence includes:

- vague intuition
- one-off impressions
- unsupported assumptions
- AI-generated claims without verification

Weak evidence may be logged, but it must be labeled as weak.

---

## Decision standard

A decision entry should answer:

- What was decided?
- Why was it decided?
- What alternatives were considered?
- What evidence supported it?
- What assumptions remain?
- What would cause the decision to be revisited?

Decisions that affect strategy, offers, platform architecture, client delivery, governance, or public positioning should be reviewed weekly.

---

## Assumption standard

An assumption should be written as a testable belief.

Poor assumption:

> SMBs want AI.

Better assumption:

> Local service businesses may buy AI implementation services when the offer is framed around reducing administrative workload rather than adopting AI tools.

Every assumption should have a possible test.

---

## Risk standard

A risk entry should identify:

- what could go wrong
- what area it affects
- how severe it is
- what signal would confirm it
- what response is available

Risks should not be hidden because they are uncomfortable. The Founder Log is a place to make risk visible before it becomes expensive.

---

## AI Cofounder standard

When writing for future AI assistance, include context that prevents hallucination or drift.

Useful AI Cofounder notes include:

- current strategy
- known constraints
- recent decisions
- unresolved questions
- personal preferences
- active risks
- forbidden assumptions
- required verification steps

Do not ask the AI Cofounder to infer critical missing context from vague notes.

---

## Safety standard

Founder Log entries must not contain:

- passwords
- API keys
- private keys
- access tokens
- unredacted client confidential information
- sensitive personal information
- private legal details not needed for business operations
- unsupported public claims
- information that should not be reused by AI

If sensitive context matters, redact it.

---

## Promotion standard

An entry may be promoted when it contains durable value.

Promote entries into:

| Destination | Promote when |
| --- | --- |
| Governance | The entry contains a decision, standard, rule, or risk response |
| Narrative | The entry contains a useful story, lesson, or positioning insight |
| Pipeline | The entry contains sales, prospect, pricing, or objection intelligence |
| Delivery | The entry contains implementation lessons or reusable workflows |
| Platform | The entry contains product, automation, or system requirements |
| Knowledge Graph | The entry contains recurring concepts, patterns, or relationships |

---

## Quality checklist

Before considering a Founder Log entry complete, check:

- Is the date clear?
- Is the summary specific?
- Are decisions separated from assumptions?
- Are risks visible?
- Are next actions clear?
- Is sensitive information redacted?
- Would this help the AI Cofounder tomorrow?
- Would this help the founder one month from now?
- Is anything worth promoting?

---

## Rule of thumb

A good Founder Log entry should make future work easier.

If it does not preserve useful context, support better decisions, or improve future execution, it probably does not belong in the Founder Log.
