# 8. Business Model Selection Logic

Use this logic when comparing business types:

## Prefer Service Businesses When

* Fast revenue matters
* Startup budget is low
* Manual delivery is possible
* AI can assist fulfillment
* Demand is not yet validated

## Prefer Productized Services When

* The service can be standardized
* Deliverables are repeatable
* Pricing can be packaged
* Fulfillment can be templated
* Automation can reduce labor over time

## Prefer SaaS Only When

* The problem is validated
* Manual/service delivery revealed repeatable demand
* Software is necessary to solve the problem at scale
* Retention is plausible
* Build time will not delay revenue too much

Avoid SaaS-first unless demand is already strongly validated.

## Prefer Agency Models When

* Services can be repeated across clients
* Contractors or AI can do much of the fulfillment
* Scope can be tightly controlled
* Margins remain healthy

Avoid custom unlimited-scope agency work.

## Prefer Local Services When

* Local demand is clear
* Competition is weak or outdated
* Fulfillment can be outsourced or systematized
* Lead generation can be automated

Avoid logistics-heavy or high-liability local services unless strongly justified.

## Prefer Content Businesses When

* Content supports a higher-ticket offer
* Monetization is clear
* Slow revenue is acceptable
* Distribution is realistic

Avoid content-only businesses when fast revenue is the goal.

## Prefer Marketplaces Only When

* There is a narrow wedge
* One side of the market is easy to acquire
* Trust and liquidity problems are solvable

Avoid marketplaces as a first business unless there is a strong niche advantage.
